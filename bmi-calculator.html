What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple measurement used to assess whether a person has a healthy body weight for their height. It is calculated using weight and height and helps categorize individuals into underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese categories.

How is BMI Calculated?

BMI is calculated using the formula:

BMI = Weight (kg) / Height (m²)

This calculator automatically converts your height from centimeters into meters and applies the formula to determine your BMI.

BMI Categories Explained

  • Underweight (Below 18.5): May indicate nutritional deficiency or other health concerns.
  • Normal (18.5 – 22.9): Healthy weight range for most individuals.
  • Overweight (23 – 24.9): Increased risk of cardiovascular issues.
  • Obese (25+): Higher risk of chronic diseases such as diabetes and hypertension.

Health Risks Associated with High BMI

A higher BMI may increase the risk of:

  • Heart disease
  • Type 2 diabetes
  • High blood pressure
  • Joint problems
  • Sleep apnea

Limitations of BMI

BMI does not directly measure body fat. Athletes or individuals with high muscle mass may have a high BMI but low body fat. Therefore, BMI should be used as a general guideline rather than a diagnostic tool.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is BMI accurate?

BMI is accurate for most people as a general health indicator but may not be perfect for athletes or bodybuilders.

How often should I check my BMI?

You can check your BMI monthly or whenever you notice significant weight changes.

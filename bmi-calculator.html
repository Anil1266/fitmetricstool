What Is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a measurement that uses your height and weight to estimate whether your body weight falls within a healthy range.

BMI is commonly used by health professionals as a quick screening tool to identify possible weight-related health risks.

BMI Categories

  • Underweight: BMI below 18.5
  • Normal weight: BMI 18.5 – 24.9
  • Overweight: BMI 25 – 29.9
  • Obesity: BMI 30 or higher

Frequently Asked Questions

What is BMI?

BMI is a number calculated from your height and weight that helps estimate whether your body weight is healthy.

Is BMI accurate for everyone?

BMI is a useful general guideline but does not consider muscle mass, body composition, or other health factors.

What is a healthy BMI range?

For most adults, a BMI between 18.5 and 24.9 is considered healthy.

What Is BMI And Why It Matters

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple measurement used to estimate whether a person's weight is healthy for their height. It is widely used by health professionals and fitness experts as a quick screening tool.

How BMI Is Calculated

BMI is calculated by dividing your weight in kilograms by the square of your height in meters.

Formula:

  • BMI = weight (kg) / height² (m²)

BMI Categories

  • Underweight: BMI below 18.5
  • Normal weight: BMI 18.5 – 24.9
  • Overweight: BMI 25 – 29.9
  • Obesity: BMI 30 or higher

Why BMI Matters

Maintaining a healthy BMI can reduce the risk of many health conditions including heart disease, diabetes, and high blood pressure.

However, BMI is only a general indicator and does not measure body fat or muscle mass directly.

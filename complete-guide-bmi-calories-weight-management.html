Complete Guide to BMI, Calories & Weight Management

Understanding your body weight is not just about appearance. It is about health, energy, and long-term well-being. This complete guide explains Body Mass Index (BMI), calorie needs, weight loss, weight gain, and hydration in simple terms.

1. What is BMI (Body Mass Index)?

BMI is a simple calculation used to estimate whether your body weight is healthy for your height.

BMI Formula:

BMI = Weight (kg) ÷ Height (m²)

Example: If your weight is 70 kg and height is 1.75 m:

  • 1.75 × 1.75 = 3.06
  • 70 ÷ 3.06 = 22.86

Your BMI is 22.86.

Global BMI Categories:

  • Below 18.5 → Underweight
  • 18.5 – 24.9 → Normal weight
  • 25 – 29.9 → Overweight
  • 30+ → Obese

2. How Many Calories Do You Need Per Day?

Calories are units of energy. Your body needs calories to breathe, move, digest food, and function daily.

Step 1: Calculate BMR

BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) is the number of calories your body burns at rest.

Step 2: Multiply by Activity Level

  • Sedentary → BMR × 1.2
  • Lightly active → BMR × 1.375
  • Moderately active → BMR × 1.55
  • Very active → BMR × 1.725

3. How to Lose Weight Safely

Weight loss happens when you create a calorie deficit — meaning you burn more calories than you consume.

1 kg of body fat ≈ 7,700 calories

To lose 5 kg, you need about 38,500 calories deficit over time.

Safe Weight Loss Rate:

  • 0.5 to 1 kg per week

Healthy Weight Loss Tips:

  • Reduce 300–500 calories daily
  • Increase physical activity
  • Eat sufficient protein
  • Sleep 7–8 hours
  • Stay hydrated

4. How to Gain Weight in a Healthy Way

Weight gain requires a calorie surplus — consuming more calories than you burn.

Healthy Weight Gain Tips:

  • Add 300–500 extra calories daily
  • Focus on protein-rich foods
  • Include healthy fats
  • Strength training exercises

5. How Much Water Should You Drink?

Hydration supports metabolism, digestion, and energy levels.

Basic Formula:

30–35 ml per kg of body weight

If you weigh 70 kg:

  • 70 × 30 = 2100 ml (2.1 liters)
  • 70 × 35 = 2450 ml (2.45 liters)

6. Why Weight Management Matters

Maintaining a healthy weight reduces risk of:

  • Heart disease
  • Type 2 diabetes
  • High blood pressure
  • Joint problems
  • Low energy levels

Balanced nutrition, consistent exercise, and regular monitoring are key to long-term health.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Is BMI accurate?

BMI is a screening tool. It does not measure body fat percentage but gives a general health estimate.

How many calories should I cut to lose weight?

A daily deficit of 300–500 calories is generally considered safe and sustainable.

Can I lose weight without exercise?

Yes, through diet control, but combining exercise improves results and muscle retention.

How long does it take to see results?

Most people see noticeable changes within 4–6 weeks of consistent effort.

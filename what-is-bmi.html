What is BMI? Complete Guide for Indian Adults

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple measurement that helps determine whether a person has a healthy body weight for their height. It is widely used by doctors, fitness professionals, and health organizations worldwide.

How is BMI Calculated?

BMI is calculated using the formula:

BMI = Weight (kg) / Height (m²)

For example, if your weight is 70 kg and your height is 1.7 meters, your BMI would be:

70 / (1.7 × 1.7) = 24.22

BMI Categories for Indian Adults

  • Below 18.5 – Underweight
  • 18.5 – 22.9 – Normal
  • 23 – 24.9 – Overweight
  • 25 and above – Obese

Indian BMI standards are slightly stricter compared to international standards because Indians are more prone to diabetes and heart-related conditions at lower BMI levels.

Why BMI is Important

Maintaining a healthy BMI can reduce the risk of:

  • Heart disease
  • Type 2 diabetes
  • High blood pressure
  • Joint problems

However, BMI does not measure body fat directly. It should be used as a screening tool, not a final diagnosis.

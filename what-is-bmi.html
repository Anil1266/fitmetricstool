What is BMI (Body Mass Index)?

BMI stands for Body Mass Index. It is a simple measurement used to estimate whether a person's weight is healthy relative to their height. BMI is widely used by health professionals to classify weight categories such as underweight, normal weight, overweight, and obesity.

BMI Formula

The formula used to calculate BMI is:

BMI = Weight (kg) / Height² (m²)

BMI Example

For example, if a person weighs 70 kg and their height is 1.75 meters:

BMI = 70 / (1.75 × 1.75)

BMI = 70 / 3.06 = 22.86

This BMI value falls within the normal weight category.

BMI Categories

  • Below 18.5 – Underweight
  • 18.5 – 24.9 – Normal weight
  • 25 – 29.9 – Overweight
  • 30 and above – Obesity

Why BMI is Important

BMI helps individuals understand whether their body weight falls within a healthy range. Although BMI does not directly measure body fat, it provides a useful screening tool for health risks associated with weight.

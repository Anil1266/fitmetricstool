Daily Water Intake Guide

Learn how hydration affects energy, fitness, recovery and overall health while understanding how much water your body needs daily.

Why Hydration Matters

Water supports essential body functions including temperature regulation, digestion, circulation and physical performance.

Proper hydration also helps maintain energy levels, concentration and recovery after exercise.

How Much Water Should You Drink?

Water requirements vary based on:

  • Body weight
  • Physical activity
  • Climate and temperature
  • Diet and lifestyle

Many people benefit from approximately 2–4 litres of water daily depending on activity levels.

How Much Water Per Kg of Body Weight?

A commonly cited guideline is about 30–35 ml of water per kilogram of body weight per day (roughly 0.5–0.6 oz per lb). For a 70 kg (154 lb) adult, that works out to roughly 2.1–2.5 litres daily, before accounting for exercise, heat, or climate — all of which increase the target.

This per-kg method is a useful starting point because it scales with body size, unlike flat "8 glasses a day" advice, though individual needs still vary with health conditions and activity.

Signs of Dehydration

  • Fatigue or low energy
  • Dry mouth
  • Dark yellow urine
  • Headaches
  • Reduced exercise performance

Hydration Tips

  • Carry a water bottle daily.
  • Drink water consistently throughout the day.
  • Increase intake during workouts.
  • Consume water-rich foods.
  • Track hydration habits regularly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I drink too much water?

Yes — excessive water intake in a short period can be harmful, so balance matters more than maximizing intake.

Does coffee count as hydration?

Coffee and tea do contribute to daily fluid intake, though plain water is still the best default choice.

How do I know if I'm dehydrated?

Common signs include fatigue, dark urine, headaches, and dry mouth — see the section above for the full list.

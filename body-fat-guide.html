Understanding Body Fat Percentage

Body fat percentage tells you more about your composition than weight alone. Here's what the numbers mean and how to measure it.

Why Body Fat % Matters More Than Weight

Two people can weigh the same but have very different body compositions. Body fat percentage separates fat mass from lean mass (muscle, bone, organs, water), giving a clearer picture of health and fitness progress than the scale alone.

How to Measure Body Fat at Home

The U.S. Navy tape-measure method uses neck, waist (and hip for women), and height to estimate body fat percentage without special equipment. It's generally within a few percentage points of more precise methods like DEXA scans, making it a practical option for tracking progress over time.

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is the U.S. Navy method?

It's generally within a few percentage points of more precise methods like DEXA scans for most people, making it a practical estimate rather than a lab-grade measurement.

What is a healthy body fat percentage?

For men, roughly 14–24% is considered acceptable to fit; for women, roughly 21–31% is considered acceptable to fit, though healthy ranges vary by age and individual.

How often should I re-measure?

Every 4–6 weeks is usually enough to see meaningful trends without over-focusing on small day-to-day fluctuations.
